Diary Plotter

A long current-clamp recording drifts — the resting potential wanders, input resistance changes, a drug goes on and off. Diary Plotter lays the whole session out as a timeline so you can see how a cell’s per-epoch measurements evolve, mark what happened when, and compare two stretches side by side. Launch it with Diary_plotter in the Command Window.

What it needs Diary Plotter reads current-clamp epoch statistics from sln_results.CCEpochStats. If a cell has no stats yet you’ll get a “No epoch results found” message — that analysis needs to run first. It’s a read-only viewer; it doesn’t change the database.

The workflow

Load a cell

Type the Cell name and click Load (optionally limit to specific Epochs). Every per-epoch statistic for that cell is pulled in and the timeline draws with epoch time on the x-axis.

Choose what to plot

The Left axis and Right axis dropdowns each pick one statistic to plot — so you can watch, say, resting potential on the left and input resistance on the right at once. Set None on the right for a single trace, and use the lower/upper fields to fix the axis ranges.

Read the drift

Steps or slow slopes in the traces are your cue — a creeping holding current or a jump in resistance flags where the recording changed or a manipulation took hold.

Mark conditions

In the Conditions panel, type a name (e.g. “washout”), click Add condition, and drag a box over the span it covers. Color and Delete condition manage them — a quick way to annotate drug windows or protocol changes over the timeline.

Compare two stretches

Draw A and Draw B let you box two epoch ranges on the diary. Their raw waveforms are drawn side by side (A and B), with parameter tables for each, so you can compare, say, before vs after a drug. The Selection A/B fields show the exact epoch ranges you boxed.
